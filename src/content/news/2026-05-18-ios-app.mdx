The Catalogue of Life is now available as a native iPhone app. You can [download it for free from the App Store](https://apps.apple.com/app/catalogue-of-life/id6769201152).

The app gives mobile access to the same authoritative checklist of every named living and recently extinct organism that powers [catalogueoflife.org](https://www.catalogueoflife.org), in a UI built for one-handed use on the go — in the field, at the museum, or on the sofa.

## What you can do

**Browse and search the full COL release.** Walk the classification tree, or search by scientific name with quick filters for rank, status and taxonomic group. Synonyms, vernacular names and authorships are all just a tap away. The Extended Release (XR) names are clearly marked, so you can tell vetted COL data apart from names contributed by additional sources.

<Image src={iosSearch} width={320} densities={[1.5, 2]} format="webp" alt="iOS app search screen" />

**Rich taxon pages.** Every taxon shows its full classification, original publication, synonyms and combinations, and an interactive sunburst breakdown of its children — handy for getting a feel for the diversity inside a genus or family at a glance.

<Image src={iosTaxon} width={320} densities={[1.5, 2]} format="webp" alt="iOS app taxon page" />

**Linked external data.** Where available, taxon pages pull in occurrence maps, country statistics and image galleries from [GBIF](https://www.gbif.org), with proper attribution and licensing carried through from the original observers. Source records link back to the contributing dataset with its citation.

<Image src={iosOccurrences} width={320} densities={[1.5, 2]} format="webp" alt="iOS app occurrence map" />

**Switch between releases.** Unlike the website, which always shows the latest data, the app lets you choose which COL release you want to work with: the latest monthly Extended release, the latest monthly Base release, or any of the annual releases going back to 2021. Useful for citing a fixed version, or for checking how a name was treated in an earlier release.

**Release metrics and sources.** The Metrics tab shows the size of the selected release and a timeline of how COL has grown over the years. The Sources tab lets you browse and filter the hundreds of taxonomic datasets that make up the Catalogue.

<Image src={iosMetrics} width={320} densities={[1.5, 2]} format="webp" alt="iOS app metrics tab" />

**Favourites.** Star any taxon to keep a personal list for quick access later.

## Requirements

The app runs on iPhone with a recent version of iOS. It is free, contains no ads, and requires no account. An Android version is not currently planned — if you would like to see one, please let us know.

We hope the app makes COL more useful in the places where a laptop isn't practical. Feedback and bug reports are very welcome via our [data issue tracker](https://github.com/CatalogueOfLife/data/issues) or by [email](mailto:support@catalogueoflife.org).
