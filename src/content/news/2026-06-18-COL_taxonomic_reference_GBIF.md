As of today [Catalogue of Life](https://www.catalogueoflife.org/) is the main taxonomic reference for the [Global Biodiversity Information Facility](https://www.GBIF.org/). GBIF launches today a new website that provides access to close to 4 billion species occurrences. These occurrences are now taxonomically organised by the Catalogue of Life. This is the result of years of effort by many people. The impact is significant. For the first time all species occurrences in GBIF are presented by a global consensus taxonomy that is updated on a monthly basis. 

Back to the year 1999. It is 3 years after the start of the Species 2000 programme with the aim to establish a catalogue of described organisms. The OECD announces the establishment of a Global Facility on Biodiversity. One of its prime content area responsibilities is establishing a global catalogue of described organisms. A collaboration with the Species 2000 programme, now no longer in existence, and later the [Integrated Taxonomic Information System](https://www.ITIS.gov/) was born. This collaboration turned into the Catalogue of Life as it is today. Congratulations GBIF with the launch of a new gbif.org!

The Catalogue of Life acknowledges and congratulates all experts who are contributing or have contributed to this global species data resource.
